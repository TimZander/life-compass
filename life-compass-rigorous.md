# Life Compass — Rigorous Edition

A more demanding version of the [5-day investigation](README.md). Same destination — a
values / passions / purpose compass — but built to resist the ways self-reflection
quietly lies to you: anchoring, social desirability, confirmation bias, bad affective
forecasting, and single-observer blindness.

**This does not replace the original.** The [original 5 days](days/) stay exactly as they
were. Use this when you want the harder, more evidence-grounded pass — a first time
through if you like a challenge, or the version you graduate to on an annual redo.

### What's different from the original

Every change here exists to close a specific gap:

| # | Change | Gap it closes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Traceability** — every value must cite Day 1 evidence | Stops "shoulds" from masquerading as values |
| 2 | **Generate before you anchor** — derive values from your data *before* seeing the word list | Anchoring / social desirability |
| 3 | **Revealed preference everywhere** — pull real calendar, money, history data | Self-report and bad future-prediction |
| 4 | **Outside input (360)** — ask people who know you | Single-observer bias |
| 5 | **Disconfirmation** — actively hunt contrary evidence | Confirmation bias |
| 6 | **Explicit intersection** — actually compute the purpose overlap | "Vibes wearing a formula" |
| 7 | **Measurement + versioning** — baseline, target, dated diffs | Can't tell growth from rationalization |

### Core track vs. Rigorous track

Rigor trades off against finishing. To protect both, items are tagged:

- **🟢 Core** — rewires an existing step, adds little or no time. Just do these.
- **🔵 Rigorous** — adds real friction (data pulls, asking other people, measurement).
  Opt in deliberately. Skipping them still leaves you better off than the original.

## Day 0 — Prep (🔵 Rigorous track, do this 3–5 days before Day 1)

The two highest-leverage additions — real data and outside input — both need lead time.
Set them in motion before you start so they're ready when you need them.

### A. Outside input — optional (closes gap #4)

> **Optional add-on.** Asking other people is the one part of this that depends on someone
> besides you, so it's been pulled out to [Optional add-ons](optional-add-ons.md) — which
> also includes a zero-outreach, from-memory version. Nothing in this edition depends on
> it; skip it freely. If you *do* want the version that asks people, it needs lead time, so
> kick it off now.

### B. Pull objective data (15 min, closes gap #3)

Gather the records that don't flatter you the way memory does. You'll mine these on Days
3 and 5:

- [ ] Export or screenshot **last month's calendar** (where time actually went)
- [ ] Pull **last 2–3 months of spending**, roughly categorized (where money actually went)
- [ ] Open your **browser / watch / listen history** and note what you return to unprompted
- [ ] Screen-time breakdown by app/category, if you have it

> Don't analyze yet. Just collect. Memory is a lossy, self-serving compressor; this is the
> uncompressed source.

## Day 1 — Excavation

Follow the [original Day 1](days/day-1-excavation.md) in full. Then add:

### 🟢 Tag your raw material (closes gap #1)

As you write, label each peak experience, low point, and energizing activity with a
one-word **quality tag** (challenge, creation, connection, mastery, service, freedom,
beauty, autonomy, …). You're building a coded dataset, not a diary. On Day 2 these tags
become the evidence behind your values.

### Optional: fold in outside input

If you did the optional outside-input add-on (even the 2-minute from-memory version), weave
it in here — see [Optional add-ons](optional-add-ons.md). Flag anything others saw that you
didn't write down yourself as "external." If you skipped it, skip this; nothing downstream
needs it.

### 🔵 Longer sampling window (reduces recency bias)

The original energy audit looks back one month — a small, possibly unrepresentative
sample. Add **5 energizing and 5 draining moments from the past *year*** as well. If the
month and the year disagree, the year is usually the truer signal.

## Day 2 — Values

> **Order change (🟢, closes gap #2):** In the original, Day 2 starts from a 70-word list,
> which anchors you toward words that merely *sound* good. Here you generate from your own
> data first, and only consult the list afterward to catch genuine gaps.

### 1. 🟢 Generate from Day 1 (15 min)

**Before looking at any list,** read back over your tagged Day 1 material. What principles
were you actually operating by in your peaks? What was violated in your low points (a
violated value is often the clearest signal of a held one)? Write 10–15 candidate values
**in your own words**, each one next to the Day 1 entry that evidences it.

| Candidate value (my words) | Day 1 evidence it comes from |
| --- | --- |
| ______ | ______ |
| ______ | ______ |
| ______ | ______ |
| ______ | ______ |
| ______ | ______ |

### 2. 🟢 Now consult the list — only to fill gaps (10 min)

*Now* open the [values list](reference/values-list.md). Add only words that name
something real you missed — not words you'd like to be true. If a word from the list has
no Day 1 evidence behind it, it does not make the cut.

### 3. The disconfirmation pass (🟢, closes gap #5)

Before narrowing, run the contrarian check on your candidates:

- **Where have I claimed a value but repeatedly acted against it when it was costly?**
  A value you betray every time it's expensive is a preference, not a value. Cross it
  out or demote it. ______
- **For my top candidates: what evidence would prove this *isn't* actually core to me?**
  If you can't name any, you haven't tested it. ______

### 4. Narrow to 10 → 5, operationalize, conflict test

Continue exactly as in the [original Day 2](days/day-2-values.md), steps 2–5 — the
force-rank to 10 then 5, the personal definitions, and the conflict test against 3 real
past decisions (the original's strongest move — keep it intact).

**Added requirement (🟢):** each of your final 5 must still cite at least one piece of
Day 1 evidence. No evidence → it's an aspiration, and it goes in a separate "aspirations"
list, honestly labeled.

## Day 3 — Passions

Follow the [original Day 3](days/day-3-passions.md). Replace its reliance on memory and
the hypothetical with evidence wherever you can.

### 🔵 Mine the real data instead of recalling it (closes gap #3)

The original's "lose track of time" and "unprompted attention" steps run on memory.
Memory cherry-picks. Use the Day 0 data instead:

- **Calendar:** which recurring blocks do you protect, arrive early to, run over on?
- **History:** what subjects do you actually return to when no one is watching? Tally them.
- **Spending:** what do you spend on without resentment, even when money is tight?
  Discretionary spend is a revealed vote.

| Source | What the data actually shows | Does it match what I'd have *guessed*? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Calendar | ______ | ______ |
| History | ______ | ______ |
| Spending | ______ | ______ |

### 🟢 Weight evidence over the hypothetical

Still do the "5 years + enough money" question — but treat it as the *weakest* input, not
the strongest. We're poor predictors of what will fulfill us. When the hypothetical and
the data disagree, **trust the data.** Note any disagreement explicitly:

> Hypothetical says ______ ; the data says ______ ; reconciling: ______

Then build your 3–5 themes as in the original — but each theme must point to at least one
**data** example, not only a remembered one.

## Day 4 — Purpose

Follow the [original Day 4](days/day-4-purpose.md) for unfair advantages, the who/what,
the contribution sentences, and the eulogy test. Add the step the original skips:

### 🟢 Actually compute the intersection (closes gap #6)

The original *defines* purpose as good-at × energizes × world-needs, then jumps straight
to drafting. Don't jump. Fill the three columns, then write only what lands in the
**overlap of all three** — that overlap is your purpose's raw ore.

| Good at (Day 4 advantages) | Energizes me (Day 3 themes) | World needs / who I'd serve |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ______ | ______ | ______ |
| ______ | ______ | ______ |
| ______ | ______ | ______ |

**In all three columns (the intersection):**

> ______

### Optional: cross-check against outside input

If you gathered outside input, compare it against your unfair-advantages list here — see
[Optional add-ons](optional-add-ons.md). Where others named a strength you *didn't*, take it
seriously: a strength so native you've stopped seeing it is often where purpose lives. Skip
otherwise.

### 🟢 Disconfirmation on the draft

For your truest purpose draft, ask: **what would I expect to see in my actual life if this
were genuinely my purpose — and do I see it?** If a purpose predicts behavior that's wholly
absent from your last five years, it's a costume, not a compass. Revise toward what the
evidence already supports.

## Day 5 — Synthesis, Measured

Assemble the compass and run the five-dimension alignment test as in the
[original Day 5](days/day-5-synthesis.md). Then make it measurable and durable.

### 🔵 Establish a real baseline (closes gap #7)

The original asks "what % of waking hours served the compass?" — make it rigorous:

1. **Define "served the compass"** before you measure, so you can't grade on a curve:
   > A block counts as aligned if it advances a top-3 value, a passion theme, or the
   > purpose. Define it: ______
2. **Measure from the Day 0 calendar**, not from memory. Aligned hours: ____ / ____ = ___%.
3. **Repeat for money:** % of discretionary spend that served the compass: ___%.
4. **Set a target** for the next quarter for each, so the quarterly review has a number to
   check against, not a vibe. Time target: ___% · Money target: ___%.

### 🔵 Version the compass (closes gap #7)

Save your finished compass as a **dated** file (e.g. `answers/compass-2026-06.md`), and
never overwrite old ones. At each annual redo, **diff against the prior version** and ask
the key question:

> Each thing that changed — is it driven by **new evidence**, or by **new
> rationalization** (talking myself into where I've drifted)? Name the cause for each change.

This is the difference between a compass that tracks your growth and one that just
ratifies your drift.

### Realignment moves + review cadence

Finish as in the original: pick 1–2 ruthless 90-day moves, and set quarterly (15 min) /
annual (90 min) review dates. The added discipline: at each quarterly review, **re-measure
the baseline numbers** and compare to target. A compass with no measurement can't tell you
whether it's working.

---

## The honest caveat

This edition asks more of you: lead time, other people, real data, and the discomfort of
hunting for evidence against your own conclusions. That friction is the price of rigor —
and it's also why fewer people finish the rigorous version than the original. **A finished
core compass beats an abandoned rigorous one.** If you're going to stall on the 🔵 steps,
do the 🟢 steps and ship it. You can always add the rigorous layer on the annual redo.
